Overview

Homogeneity

$\alpha$ is an automorphism of $\mathcal A$ if for every relation $R^{\mathcal A}$ and $\overline a\in A^n$,

$\overline a\in R^{\mathcal A}\Longleftrightarrow \alpha(\overline a)\in R^{\mathcal A}$.

$\Aut(\mathcal A)$: set of automorphisms of the structure $\mathcal A$

Definition: $\mathcal A$ is homogeneous if every partial isomorphism $\mathcal A\to\mathcal A$ with finite domain extends to an automorphism.

Uniquely identified by the set $\Age(\mathcal A)$ of its finite substructures. [FraÃ¯ssÃ© '54]

Examples:
  • $(\Bbb Q; <)$ is homogeneous; its finite substructures are precisely the finite linear orders.
  • The Rado graph (finite simple undirected graphs).
  • The Henson graph $H_3$ (finite undirected triangle-free graphs).

Definition: $\mathcal A$ is finitely bounded if one can describe its substructures by finitely many forbidden patterns.

Clones

A clone is a set $\mathscr C$ of finitary operations on a set $A$ containing the projections and closed under composition.

$f\colon\mathcal A^n\to\mathcal A$ is a polymorphism of $\mathcal A$ if it preserves every relation of $\mathcal A$.

\begin{matrix} (a^1_1 & a^1_2 & \dots & a^1_m) & \in R^{\mathcal A}\\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots & \\ (a^n_1 & a^n_2 & \dots & a^n_m) & \in R^{\mathcal A}\\ & & \Downarrow& & \\ (f(a^1_1,\dots,a^n_1) & f(a^1_2,\dots,a^n_2) & \dots & f(a^1_m,\dots,a^n_m)) & \in R^{\mathcal A} \end{matrix}

$\Pol(\mathcal A)$ is a clone.

If $\mathcal A$ is $\omega$-categorical, then $\langle\mathcal A\rangle = \Inv(\Pol(\mathcal A))$.
[Geiger '68; Bodnarčuk, Kalužnin, Kotov, Romov '69; Bodirsky, Nešetřil '06]
$\leadsto$ For $\omega$-categorical structures, study of coclones = study of clones.

Canonical Functions

$f\colon B^n\to B$ is canonical with respect to $\mathcal B$ if one of the following equivalent conditions hold:
  • for all $\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_n\in\Aut(\mathcal B)$, $f\circ(\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_n)\in\overline{\{\beta\circ f\mid \beta\in\Aut(\mathcal B)\}}$,
  • $f$ induces a function $f^{\text{orb}}_m$ on orbits of $m$-tuples under $\Aut(\mathcal B)$.
$\leadsto$ For a clone containing $\Aut(\mathcal B)$, let $\Aut(\mathcal B)\subset \mathscr C_{can} \subseteq \mathscr C$ be the canonical subclone of $\mathscr C$ consisting of functions that are canonical with respect to $\mathcal B$.
$\mathcal A$ definable in a finitely bounded homogeneous structure $\mathcal B$.
If $\Pol_{can}(\mathcal A)$ has no uniformly continuous homomorphism to $\mathscr P$, then $\Csp(\mathcal A)$ is in P.
[Bodirsky, M '16]
$\Rightarrow$ The Bodirsky-Pinsker conjecture holds for clones of canonical functions.
$\Rightarrow$ Unification of several tractability results.

The Homomorphism Extension Problem

Recall:
  • If $\Pol_{can}(\mathcal A)\not\rightarrow\mathscr P$, $\Csp(\mathcal A)$ is in P.
  • If $\Pol(\mathcal A)\rightarrow\mathscr P$, $\Csp(\mathcal A)$ is NP-hard.
Does every homomorphism $\Pol_{can}(\mathcal A)\rightarrow\mathscr P$ extend to $\Pol(\mathcal A)\rightarrow\mathscr P$?
Suppose that $\mathcal B$ is a Ramsey structure. Let $f\colon B^n\to B$.
There is in $\overline{\Aut(\mathcal B)f\Aut(\mathcal B)}$ a function that is canonical with respect to $\mathcal B$.
[Bodirsky, Pinsker, Tsankov '12]
A solution to the homomorphism extension problem..?
  • Assume $\xi\colon\Pol_{can}(\mathcal A)\rightarrow\mathscr P$ is a homomorphism.
  • For $f\in\Pol(\mathcal A)$, define $\tilde{\xi}(f)$ to be $\xi(g)$, where $g$ is any canonical friend of $f$.
In general, $\tilde{\xi}$ is not well-defined and the homomorphism extension problem has a negative answer.

Positive answers to HEP

The Homomorphism Extension Problem has a positive answer in the following cases:
  • Reducts of unary structures: the mashup technique, [Bodirsky, M '18]
  • MMSNP: hybrid relational approach. [Bodirsky, Madelaine, M '18]
Question: For which structures $\mathcal B$ does HEP have a positive answer?

MMSNP

Input: a finite graph $G$,
Question: can $G$ be colored with and in a way to avoid monochromatic triangles?
Complexity: NP-hard.
Input: a finite graph $G$,
Question: can $G$ be colored with and in a way to avoid the following patterns?
Complexity: in P.
In general, coloring problems with forbidden patterns.
The no-monochromatic-triangle problem is not a finite-domain CSP.
Every finite-domain CSP can be seen as such a coloring problem.
Every coloring problem is equivalent to a finite-domain CSP.
[Feder, Vardi '93; Kun '13]
Every MMSNP problem is $\Csp(\mathcal A)$ for some $\mathcal A$ in the Bodirsky-Pinsker class.
[Bodirsky, Dalmau '12]
Construction based on universal $\omega$-categorical structures.
[Cherlin, Shelah, Shi '98; Hubička, Nešetřil '16]
The Homomorphism Extension Problem has a positive answer for CSS structures.
[Bodirsky, Madelaine, M '18]
$\Rightarrow$ The Bodirsky-Pinsker conjecture is true for CSPs in MMSNP.
$\Rightarrow$ New proof of the Feder-Vardi-Kun result.
