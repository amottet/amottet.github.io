CSPs with Finite Templates

Universal algebra $\leftrightarrow$ Constraint Satisfaction

In this talk, "structure" means relational structure $\mathbb A=(A;R_1,\dots,R_k)$
$\Hom(\mathbb A)$ is the problem:
  • Input: $\mathbb X$, finite
  • Question: does there exist a homomorphism $\mathbb X\to\mathbb A$?
same as defining the set of yes-instances $\Hom(\mathbb A)=\{\mathbb X\mid \mathbb X \text{ finite structure s.t. } \mathbb X\to\mathbb A\}$
  • Many natural classes are of the form $\Hom(\mathbb A)$: graphs with chromatic number at most $k$, graphs with clique number at most $k$, acyclic digraphs, ...
  • Complexity of $\Hom(\mathbb A)\; =$ complexity of deciding membership of a given $\mathbb X$ in such classes
  • One can have $\mathbb A$ infinite. Sometimes it must be infinite.
The class of finite loopless graphs is $\Hom(\mathbb K_\infty)$, where $\mathbb K_\infty$ is the countable complete graph.
If $\{\text{finite loopless graphs}\}\subseteq\Hom(\mathbb A)$ and $\mathbb A$ has fewer than $n$ vertices, then since $K_n$ is loopless and $K_n\to\mathbb A$, $\mathbb A$ must contain a loop, so $\Hom(\mathbb A)$ contains all finite graphs.

CSPs with $\omega$-categorical templates

Infinite CSPs and Oligomorphic Clones

No hope to understand all CSPs in general.
Restrict to $\CSP(\mathbb A)$ where $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ is well-behaved.
A clone $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ on $A$ is oligomorphic if all the finite powers $A^n$ are finitely generated already under the invertible unary operations.
Equivalently, the group $\Aut(\mathbb A)$ acts with finitely many orbits on every $A^n$.
  • Finite $A$
  • $\Pol(\mathbb A) \supseteq $ all permutations on $\mathbb Q$, or all monotone permutations on $\mathbb Q$
  • $\Pol(\mathbb A) \supseteq$ automorphism group of a homogeneous structure (like $(\mathbb Q;<)$ or the Rado graph)
Consequences:
  • Finitely many $\Pol(\mathbb A)$-invariant subsets of $A^n$ for all $n$.
  • By compactness/induction arguments, can recover some properties that hold for finite clones (Pol-Inv correspondence).
  • Define $f \sim g$ iff for every finite $S\subseteq A$, there exists $\alpha\in\mathcal G$ such that $\alpha f|_S=g|_S$.
    Then $(A^{A^n})/{\sim}$ is compact for all $n$.
  • Corresponds to $\mathbb A$ being $\omega$-categorical. [Ryll-Nardzewski, Engeler, Svenonius]

Promise CSPs with Finite Templates

Promise CSPs

$\mathbb A, \mathbb B$ structures with $\mathbb A\to\mathbb B$.
$\PCSP(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is the problem of determining, given $\mathbb X$, whether
  • Yes: there exists a homomorphism $\mathbb X\to\mathbb A$
  • No: there does not exist a homomorphism $\mathbb X\to\mathbb B$
Since $\mathbb A\to\mathbb B$, at most one case is possible.
We are promised that at least one is true.
  • $\mathbb {OIT}=(\{0,1\}, \{(1,0,0),(0,1,0),(0,0,1)\})$ vs. $\mathbb{NAE}_2=(\{0,1\}, \{0,1\}^3\setminus\{(0,0,0),(1,1,1)\})$
    Solvable in polynomial time!
  • $\PCSP(\mathbb K_a,\mathbb K_b)$ for $a\leq b$
    Conjectured to be NP-hard for all $a\leq b$ (known e.g. for $a=3\leq b\leq 6$)
    Does not pp-construct $\mathbb K_3$ in general.

About PCSPs

  • Complexity determined by polymorphisms $\mathbb A^n\to\mathbb B$
    $\Pol(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is a minion: set of functions closed under minors
    if $f\colon \mathbb A^n\to \mathbb B$ is a polymorphism and $\sigma\colon\{1,\dots,n\}\to\{1,\dots,m\}$, $f^{\sigma}(x_1,\dots,x_m) := f(x_{\sigma(1)},\dots,x_{\sigma(n)})$
    is a polymorphism.
  • $\PCSP(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is equivalent to the following problem (for some constant $N$):
    Given $\Sigma$ a set of height 1 identities about functions of arity at most $N$, decide whether
    • Yes: the identities are satisfied by projections on a 2-element set
    • No: the identities are not satisfiable in $\Pol(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$
  • $(\mathbb C,\mathbb D)$ pp-constructible in $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ iff $\Pol(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)\to\Pol(\mathbb C,\mathbb D)$
    Implies $\PCSP(\mathbb C,\mathbb D)\leq \PCSP(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$.
  • Hardness not well understood
    (every year comes with a stronger kind of hardness proof + proof that previous techniques are not enough)
  • Fertile ground for new algorithms feeding back into world of finite-domain CSPs
    Basic Linear Programming relaxation (BLP) and Affine Integer Programming relaxation (AIP)

Bounded Width and Finite Tractability

  • $\mathbb X$ is locally consistent w.r.t. $\mathbb A$ if no contradiction can be derived by looking at constant-sized subsets of $\mathbb X$ and propagating implied constraints
  • $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ has bounded width if every $\mathbb X$ that is locally consistent w.r.t. $\mathbb A$ admits a homomorphism to $\mathbb B$
  • Makes sense for CSPs as well (with $\mathbb A=\mathbb B$)
  • If $\mathbb A$ finite, then $\mathbb A$ has bounded width iff $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ contains WNU of all arities [Larose, Zádori] [Maróti, McKenzie] [Barto, Kozik]
Classify the finite PCSP templates $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ that have bounded width.

  • $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is finitely tractable if there exists a finite $\mathbb C$ such that $$\mathbb A\to\mathbb C\to\mathbb B$$ such that $\Hom(\mathbb C)$ is solvable in polynomial time
  • The template $(\mathbb{OIT}, \mathbb{NAE}_2)$ is not finitely tractable, although the PCSP is solvable in polynomial time [Barto]

Oligomorphic clones as limits of finite minions

For $m$ large enough, define $\Gamma_m\colon \mathbb A=(A;R_1,\dots,R_k) \mapsto \Gamma_m\mathbb A$ structure on $A^m$ with:
  • unary relations encoding $R_1,\dots,R_k$
  • binary relations specifying equalities between $m$-tuples
Let $\mathcal G$ be a subgroup of $\mathbb B$. A map $\xi\colon\mathcal M\to\Pol(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is a $\mathcal G$-weak minion homomorphism if for every height 1 identity $f(\text{vars})= g(\text{vars})$ in $\mathcal M$, the identity $u\circ \xi(f)(\text{vars})=v\circ\xi(g)(\text{vars})$ holds for some $u,v\in\overline{\mathcal G}$.

Oligomorphic clones as limits of finite minions

  • For a reduct $\mathbb A$ of a finitely bounded homogeneous structure, $\Hom(\mathbb A)$ is equivalent to $\PCSP(\Gamma_m\mathbb A,\Gamma_m\mathbb A/{\mathcal G})$ (for some $m\in\mathbb N$ and $\mathcal G\subseteq\Aut(\mathbb A)$)
  • The height 1 identities that hold in $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ modulo $\mathcal G$ are those that hold in every $\Pol(\Gamma_m\mathbb A_i,\Gamma_m\mathbb A/{\mathcal G})$.
    • $\mathcal M$ be the free minion generated by identity
    • If $\mathcal M\to(\Gamma_m\mathbb A_i,\Gamma_m\mathbb A/{\mathcal G})$ for all $m, i$ then $\mathcal M\color{red}{\to}\Pol(\mathbb A)$ by previous result
  • If $\mathbb A$ does not pp-construct $(\mathbb K_3,\mathbb K_r)$, then $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ contains a pseudo-Siggers operation.
    • $(\Gamma_m\mathbb A_i,\Gamma_m\mathbb A/{\mathcal G})$ does not pp-construct $(\mathbb K_3,\mathbb K_r)$ for all $m, i$
    • $\Pol(\Gamma_m\mathbb A_i,\Gamma_m \mathbb A/{\mathcal G})$ contains a Siggers operation [Barto, Bulín, Krokhin, Opršal]
  • If $\mathbb A$ does not pp-construct $(\mathbb{NAE}_2,\mathbb{NAE}_r)$, then $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ contains a pseudo-Olšák operation $$u\circ f(x,x,y,y,y,x) \approx v\circ f(x,y,x,y,x,y)\approx w\circ f(y,x,x,x,y,y).$$
  • If $\mathbb A$ has bounded width, $\Pol(\mathbb A)$ contains pseudo weak near-unanimity operations of all arities [Atserias, Dalmau] $$u_1\circ f(x,x,\dots,x,y)\approx u_2\circ f(x,x,\dots,y,x)\approx\dots\approx u_{k-1}\circ f(x,y,\dots,x,x)\approx u_k\circ f(y,x,\dots,x,x).$$

$\mathcal G$-weak minion homomorphisms

Let $\mathcal G$ be an oligomorphic subgroup of $\Aut(\mathbb B)$. The following are equivalent:
  1. There exists a uniformly continuous $\mathcal G$-weak minion homomorphism $\Pol(\mathbb A)\to\Pol(\mathbb B)$
  2. $\mathbb A$ pp-constructs $(\Gamma_m\mathbb C,\Gamma_m\mathbb B/{\mathcal G})$ for all $m\in\mathbb N$ and finite substructure $\mathbb C$ of $\mathbb B$.
No such characterization for "$\mathbb A$ pp-constructs $\mathbb B$" or $\Pol(\mathbb A)\to\Pol(\mathbb B)$ is known, only the following:
Let $\mathbb B$ be $\omega$-categorical. Suppose that there exists a uniformly continuous minion homomorphism $\Pol(\mathbb A)\to\Pol(\mathbb B)$. Then $\mathbb A$ pp-constructs $(\mathbb C,\mathbb B)$ for every finite substructure $\mathbb C$ of $\mathbb B$. [Barto, Opršal, Pinsker] [Barto, Bulín, Krokhin, Opršal]

[M.]
There exists a PCSP template $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ such that:
  • $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ has bounded width,
  • $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is not finitely tractable,
  • $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ is not solvable by BLP+AIP,
  • There exists an $\omega$-categorical $\mathbb C$ such that $\mathbb A\to\mathbb C\to\mathbb B$ and $\CSP(\mathbb C)$ is solvable in polynomial time.
Same holds with "solvable by singleton AIP" instead of bounded width.
  • If $(\mathbb A,\mathbb B)$ finitely tractable, it has cyclic polymorphisms of all large prime arities [Barto, Kozik]
  • Let $\mathbb C=(\mathbb Q;R,\neq)$ where $R=\{(a,b,c)\in\mathbb Q^3\mid a=b\Rightarrow a\geq c\}$
  • Take $\mathbb C'$ to be a $(\geq 2)$-element substructure
  • $\Pol(\mathbb C',\mathbb C)$ does not satisfy any cyclic term condition modulo $\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)$
  • $\Pol(\Gamma_m\mathbb C',\Gamma_m\mathbb C/{\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)})$ contains no cyclic operation for some $m$ and finite $\mathbb C'\subseteq\mathbb C$
    $\Longrightarrow (\Gamma_m\mathbb C',\Gamma_m\mathbb C/{\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)})$ is not finitely tractable
  • $\Gamma_m\mathbb C'\to\Gamma_m\mathbb C\to \Gamma_m\mathbb C/{\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)}$ and $\Gamma_m\mathbb C$ is $\omega$-categorical, its CSP is in P
  • $(\Gamma_m\mathbb C',\Gamma_m\mathbb C/{\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)})$ has bounded width (next slide)

Let $\mathbb B$ be such that $\Aut(\mathbb B)\supseteq\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)$. Suppose that $\CSP(\mathbb B)$ is not NP-hard. Then $\Pol(\mathbb B)$ contains some function $f$ preserving $\theta:=(x=0\Leftrightarrow y=0)$ on $\mathbb Q_{\geq 0}$ and such that $f/{\theta}$ is cyclic.
Proof uses a loop lemma. Every $\emptyset\neq R\subseteq \mathbb Q^2$ invariant under $\Pol(\mathbb B)$ contains a pseudo-loop modulo $\Aut(\mathbb Q;<,0)$.
$\Rightarrow$ Let $\mathbb A\subseteq\mathbb B$ be finite and $\theta$ isolate the minimal element of $\mathbb A$. Then $\Pol(\mathbb A/{\theta})$ is a non-trivial Boolean clone.
Let $\mathbb X$ be an input that is locally consistent and passes the singleton AIP test as an instance of $\CSP(\mathbb A)$. Let $\theta$ be the congruence on $\mathbb A$ isolating the minimal element $a$.
  1. $\mathbb X$ is locally consistent/has singleton AIP witness for $\mathbb A/{\theta}$
  2. Consistency+AIP witnesses the existence of a nontrivial homomorphism $h^{-1}\colon\mathbb X\to\mathbb A/{\theta}$
  3. Repeat previous step on $\mathbb X\setminus h^{-1}(a)$*
  • "New" algorithm for $\Hom(\mathbb B)$ when $\Aut(\mathbb B)\supseteq\Aut(\mathbb Q;<)$!
  • Algorithm is uniform and does not depend on $\Pol(\mathbb B)$
  • Only proof of correctness uses polymorphisms
  • Iterative process as for finite CSPs
  • (Re)moves an important obstacle: the need for ad hoc algorithms solving CSPs over $(\mathbb Q;<)$.
